## 1. Concept of Return

- **Return** is the gain or loss on an investment over a specific period of time.
- It is expressed as a **percentage** of the initial investment.
- Return consists of:
  - **Income:** Interest, dividends, or rental income received.
  - **Capital Gain (or Loss):** The change in the price of the asset.

**Formula – Holding Period Return (HPR):**

$$ HPR = \frac{\text{Ending Value} - \text{Beginning Value} + \text{Income}}{\text{Beginning Value}} $$

$$ \text{Return } \% = HPR \times 100 $$

---

### Solved Numerical 1 (Direct from PDF)

**Question:** An investor buys a share for Rs. 1,000. After one year, the price becomes Rs. 1,150 and a dividend of Rs. 50 is received. Calculate the return.

**Step 1:** Identify data.
- Beginning Value = 1,000
- Ending Value = 1,150
- Income = 50

**Step 2:** Apply formula.
$$ HPR = \frac{1150 - 1000 + 50}{1000} = \frac{200}{1000} = 0.20 $$

**Step 3:** Convert to percentage.
$$ \text{Return} = 0.20 \times 100 = 20\% $$

> **Final Answer: 20%**

---

### Solved Numerical 2 (Exam Variation – Finding Missing Price)

**Question:** A stock was purchased for $50. During the year, it paid a $2 dividend. If the total return was 14%, what was the selling price?

**Step 1:** Let ending value = $P$.
**Step 2:** Formula: $(P - 50 + 2) / 50 = 0.14$
**Step 3:** $P - 48 = 7 \implies P = 55$

> **Final Answer: $55**

## 2. Concept of Risk

- **Risk** is the possibility that the **actual return** will differ from the **expected return**.
- Risk represents **uncertainty** of returns.
- **Higher uncertainty = Higher risk.**

---

## 3. Types of Risk (The Core Theory)

*(Your PDF lists 6 types. In university exams, you must also classify them as Systematic or Unsystematic).*

### Systematic Risk (Market Risk – Non-Diversifiable)
Risk inherent to the **entire market**. Cannot be eliminated by holding a diversified portfolio.
- **Market Risk:** Fluctuations due to stock market movements.
- **Inflation Risk:** Loss of purchasing power.
- **Interest Rate Risk:** Changes in interest rates affecting bond prices and borrowing costs.

### Unsystematic Risk (Firm-Specific – Diversifiable)
Risk unique to a **specific company or industry**. Can be eliminated through diversification.
- **Business Risk:** Risk due to business operations (competition, demand changes).
- **Financial Risk:** Risk due to debt financing (fixed interest payments).
- **Liquidity Risk:** Risk that an asset cannot be sold quickly without a discount.

## 4. Expected Return

- Expected return is the **weighted average** of all possible returns, using probabilities as weights.

**Formula:**

$$ E(R) = \sum (R_i \times P_i) $$

---

### Solved Numerical 3 (Direct from PDF)

**Question:** A stock has the following possible returns and probabilities:

| Return (%) | Probability |
| :--- | :--- |
| 10% | 0.2 |
| 15% | 0.5 |
| 20% | 0.3 |

Find the expected return.

**Step 1:** Multiply each return by its probability.
- $10\% \times 0.2 = 2.0\%$
- $15\% \times 0.5 = 7.5\%$
- $20\% \times 0.3 = 6.0\%$

**Step 2:** Sum them up.
$$ E(R) = 2.0 + 7.5 + 6.0 = 15.5\% $$

> **Final Answer: 15.5%**

---

### Solved Numerical 4 (Exam Variation – Missing Probability)

**Question:** A stock has 20% chance of 5% return, 50% chance of 10% return, and the rest chance of 15% return. Find E(R).

**Step 1:** Missing probability = $1 - 0.2 - 0.5 = 0.3$
**Step 2:** $E(R) = 0.2(5) + 0.5(10) + 0.3(15) = 1 + 5 + 4.5 = 10.5\%$

> **Final Answer: 10.5%**

## 5. Measurement of Risk (Variance, SD, CV)

### Standard Deviation ($\sigma$)
Measures the **dispersion** of possible returns around the expected return. **Higher $\sigma$ = Higher risk.**

**Formula:**

$$ \sigma = \sqrt{\sum (R_i - E(R))^2 \times P_i} $$

### Variance ($\sigma^2$)
The square of standard deviation.

$$ \sigma^2 = \sum (R_i - E(R))^2 \times P_i $$

### Coefficient of Variation (CV)
Measures **risk per unit of return**. Used to compare investments with different expected returns.

$$ CV = \frac{\sigma}{E(R)} $$

**Rule:** Lower CV is better (less risk for the same return).

---

### Solved Numerical 5 (Direct from PDF – SD & Variance)

**Question:** Using the same data as before ($E(R) = 15.5\%$):

| Return ($R_i$) | Probability ($P_i$) |
| :--- | :--- |
| 10% | 0.2 |
| 15% | 0.5 |
| 20% | 0.3 |

Calculate the Variance and Standard Deviation.

---

**Step 1:** Find deviations from $E(R) = 15.5\%$:
- $10 - 15.5 = -5.5$
- $15 - 15.5 = -0.5$
- $20 - 15.5 = 4.5$

**Step 2:** Square the deviations:
- $(-5.5)^2 = 30.25$
- $(-0.5)^2 = 0.25$
- $(4.5)^2 = 20.25$

**Step 3:** Multiply by probabilities and sum (Variance):
$$ \sigma^2 = 30.25(0.2) + 0.25(0.5) + 20.25(0.3) $$
$$ \sigma^2 = 6.05 + 0.125 + 6.075 = 12.25 $$

**Step 4:** Standard Deviation:
$$ \sigma = \sqrt{12.25} = 3.5\% $$

> **Final Answer:** Variance = **12.25**, Standard Deviation = **3.5%**

---

### Solved Numerical 6 (Direct from PDF – Coefficient of Variation)

**Question:** Using the above data ($\sigma = 3.5\%$, $E(R) = 15.5\%$), find CV.

$$ CV = \frac{3.5}{15.5} = 0.226 $$

> **Final Answer: 0.226** (This investment has moderate risk per unit of return).

---

### Solved Numerical 7 (Exam-Level – Comparing Two Investments)

**Question:** Stock A has $E(R) = 18\%$, $\sigma = 9\%$. Stock B has $E(R) = 12\%$, $\sigma = 5\%$. Which stock is riskier on a relative basis?

**Step 1:** Calculate CV for both.
- $CV_A = 9 / 18 = 0.50$
- $CV_B = 5 / 12 = 0.42$

**Step 2:** Compare. $CV_B$ is lower, so Stock B has **less risk per unit of return**.

> **Final Answer:** Stock B is better on a risk-adjusted basis.

## 6. Risk–Return Relationship (The Golden Rule)

- There is a **direct relationship** between risk and return.
- **Higher risk $\rightarrow$ Higher expected return**
- **Lower risk $\rightarrow$ Lower expected return**
- Investors choose investments based on their **risk tolerance**.

---

## 7. Engineering Economics Perspective

- Risk affects **capital budgeting decisions** (which projects to accept).
- Used in **project evaluation** (NPV, IRR become risky if cash flows are uncertain).
- Important in **cost–benefit analysis**.
- Helps engineers select **optimal projects** by balancing risk and return.

## 12. Common Mistakes (Exam Traps)

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Approach |
| :--- | :--- |
| Forgetting to add Income (dividend) in HPR. | Always do $EV - BV + Income$. |
| Using probabilities that don't sum to 1.0. | Check $\sum P_i = 1$. If not, adjust. |
| Squaring the return instead of the deviation. | Square $(R_i - E(R))$, not $R_i$. |
| Confusing Variance with Standard Deviation. | SD = $\sqrt{\text{Variance}}$. |
| Using CV to measure total risk. | CV measures risk *per unit*, not total risk. |
| Calling Systematic risk "diversifiable". | Systematic = Non-diversifiable. Unsystematic = Diversifiable. |
