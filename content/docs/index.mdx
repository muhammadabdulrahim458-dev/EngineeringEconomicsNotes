## 1. What are they? (Simple Definitions)

| Subject | Simple Definition |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Accounting** | The systematic recording, summarising, and reporting of financial transactions. |
| **Finance** | The management of money, investments, and financial risks — looking forward to create value. |
| **Economics** | The study of how individuals and societies allocate scarce resources to satisfy unlimited wants. |

---

## 2. Purpose & Why We Study Them

| Subject | Primary Purpose | Why Engineers Study It |
|---------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Accounting** | Provide an accurate historical record of financial performance and position. | To read financial statements, estimate costs, and track project budgets. |
| **Finance** | Make decisions about raising and using money to maximise value. | To evaluate project profitability, compare investment alternatives, and manage capital. |
| **Economics** | Understand how markets, incentives, and trade‑offs affect decisions. | To consider opportunity costs, market conditions, and resource allocation in engineering projects. |

---

## 3. Core Concepts in Each Field

### 📘 Accounting
- **Financial Statements**  
  - *Balance Sheet*: Assets = Liabilities + Equity (snapshot at a point in time)  
  - *Income Statement*: Revenue – Expenses = Net Income (performance over a period)  
  - *Cash Flow Statement*: Shows cash inflows/outflows from operating, investing, financing activities.
- **Cost Concepts**: Fixed vs. Variable, Direct vs. Indirect, Sunk vs. Opportunity costs.
- **Depreciation**: Allocating asset cost over its useful life.

### 📗 Finance
- **Time Value of Money**: A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow.
- **Capital Budgeting**: Evaluating long‑term investments (NPV, IRR, Payback).
- **Risk & Return**: Higher potential returns come with higher risk.
- **Financial Ratios**: Profitability (ROE, ROA), Liquidity (Current Ratio), Leverage (Debt‑to‑Equity).

### 📙 Economics
- **Microeconomics**: Decision‑making of individuals and firms – supply/demand, pricing, competition.
- **Macroeconomics**: Economy‑wide factors – inflation, interest rates, GDP, employment.
- **Key Economic Concepts**:  
  - *Opportunity Cost* – the value of the next best alternative foregone.  
  - *Marginal Analysis* – decisions based on incremental costs and benefits.  
  - *Market Equilibrium* – where supply meets demand.

## 4. How They Differ (Exam Favourite)

| Aspect | Accounting | Finance | Economics |
|--------|------------|---------|-----------|
| **Focus** | Past (historical) | Future (forward‑looking) | Broad (individual & societal choices) |
| **Primary Concern** | Recording & reporting | Decision‑making & value creation | Resource allocation & trade‑offs |
| **Key Output** | Financial statements | Investment/financing decisions | Market analysis & policy recommendations |
| **Time Horizon** | Periodic (monthly, annually) | Short‑term to long‑term | Often long‑term / structural |
| **Risk** | Not actively managed | Central to finance | Analysed as part of choices |
| **Measurement** | Monetary, historical cost | Cash flows, market values | Real resources, opportunity costs |

---

## 5. How They Connect in Engineering Economics

Engineering Economics sits at the intersection of all three:

- **Accounting** provides the **data**: project costs, depreciation, historical financials.
- **Finance** provides the **methods**: NPV, IRR, payback, risk analysis.
- **Economics** provides the **logic**: scarcity, opportunity cost, market conditions, cost‑benefit thinking.

When an engineer evaluates a new factory, they:
- Use **accounting** to know labour, material, and overhead costs.
- Use **finance** to calculate if the project returns more than the cost of capital.
- Use **economics** to consider the opportunity cost of alternative uses of land/money, and to forecast market demand.

## 6. Quick Summary Table – When to Use What

| Situation | Which Discipline is Most Relevant? |
|-----------|------------------------------------|
| Recording daily expenses | **Accounting** |
| Deciding between two machines | **Finance** (capital budgeting) |
| Understanding why raw material prices rose | **Economics** (supply/demand) |
| Preparing a project budget | **Accounting** + **Finance** |
| Assessing the cost of not taking a project | **Economics** (opportunity cost) |

## 7. Exam‑Style Definitions You Must Memorise

- **Accounting**: *“The process of identifying, measuring, recording, and communicating economic information to permit informed judgments and decisions.”*
- **Finance**: *“The art and science of managing money – encompassing investment, financing, and dividend decisions.”*
- **Economics**: *“The study of how people and society choose to employ scarce resources that could have alternative uses.”*
