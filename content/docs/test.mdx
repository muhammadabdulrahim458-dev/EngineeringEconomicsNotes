## 1. Core Idea

* **Time Value of Money (TVM):** A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow because it can earn interest.

* **Two main operations:**

  * **Compounding** → Find Future Value (FV)
  * **Discounting** → Find Present Value (PV)

---


## 1. Ordinary Annuity

An ordinary annuity involves periodic payments made at the **end** of each period (e.g., end of the month or year).

* **Future Value ($F.V$):**

$$F.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ (1 + r)^n - 1 \right]$$


* **Present Value ($P.V$):**

$$P.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ 1 - \frac{1}{(1 + r)^n} \right]$$



---

## 2. Annuity Due

An annuity due involves periodic payments made at the **beginning** of each period. Because each payment has more time to earn interest, you multiply the ordinary annuity formulas by $(1 + r)$.

* **Future Value ($F.V$):**

$$F.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ (1 + r)^n - 1 \right] (1 + r)$$


* **Present Value ($P.V$):**

$$P.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ 1 - \frac{1}{(1 + r)^n} \right] (1 + r)$$



---

### Variable Key:

* **$C$** = Periodic cash flow (payment amount)
* **$r$** = Interest rate per period
* **$n$** = Total number of periods



## 2. Essential Formulas

| Concept                     | Formula                                  |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Future Value (Lump Sum)     | $FV = PV(1+i)^n$                         |
| Present Value (Lump Sum)    | $PV = \frac{FV}{(1+i)^n}$                |
| FV of Ordinary Annuity      | $FV = PMT \times \frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}$    |
| PV of Ordinary Annuity      | $PV = PMT \times \frac{1-(1+i)^{-n}}{i}$ |
| Annuity Due                 | Ordinary Annuity $\times (1+i)$          |
| Perpetuity                  | $PV = \frac{PMT}{i}$                     |
| Effective Annual Rate (EAR) | $EAR = \left(1+\frac{r}{m}\right)^m-1$   |
| Continuous Compounding      | $FV = PV \times e^{rt}$                  |

---

## 3. Key Distinctions (Exam Favourites)

| Term              | Meaning                                               |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Simple Interest   | Interest only on principal. $I = P \times r \times t$ |
| Compound Interest | Interest on principal + accumulated interest          |
| Ordinary Annuity  | Payments occur at the **end** of each period          |
| Annuity Due       | Payments occur at the **beginning** of each period    |
| Nominal Rate      | Stated annual rate                                    |
| Effective Rate    | Actual annual return after compounding                |

---

## 4. Quick Problem-Solving Steps

1. Draw a timeline.
2. Identify the cash flow pattern.
3. Check whether it is an ordinary annuity or annuity due.
4. Match periods correctly.
5. Select the formula.
6. Substitute values.
7. Solve.

## 5. Must-Know Examples

### Example 1: Future Value

**Question**

Invest $1000 at 6% compounded annually for 5 years.

**Solution**

$$
FV = 1000(1.06)^5
$$

$$
FV = 1000(1.3382)
$$

$$
FV = 1338.23
$$

> **Final Answer: $1338.23**

---

### Example 2: Present Value

**Question**

How much should you invest today to get $5000 in 4 years at 7%?

**Solution**

$$
PV = \frac{5000}{(1.07)^4}
$$

$$
PV = \frac{5000}{1.3108}
$$

$$
PV = 3814.46
$$

> **Final Answer: $3814.46**

---

### Example 3: Future Value of Ordinary Annuity

**Question**

Deposit $200 at the end of each year for 3 years at 5%.

**Solution**

$$
FV = 200 \times \frac{(1.05)^3-1}{0.05}
$$

$$
FV = 200 \times 3.1525
$$

$$
FV = 630.50
$$

> **Final Answer: $630.50**

---

### Example 4: Present Value of Ordinary Annuity

**Question**

Receive $500 every year for 4 years at 6%.

**Solution**

$$
PV = 500 \times \frac{1-(1.06)^{-4}}{0.06}
$$

$$
PV = 500 \times 3.4651
$$

$$
PV = 1732.55
$$

> **Final Answer: $1732.55**

---

### Example 5: Loan Payment

**Question**

Borrow $8000 at 5% for 3 years.

**Solution**

$$
PMT = \frac{8000 \times 0.05}{1-(1.05)^{-3}}
$$

$$
PMT = \frac{400}{0.1362}
$$

$$
PMT = 2938.64
$$

> **Final Answer: $2938.64 per year**

---

### Example 6: Effective Annual Rate

**Question**

8% compounded quarterly. Find EAR.

**Solution**

$$
EAR = \left(1+\frac{0.08}{4}\right)^4-1
$$

$$
EAR = (1.02)^4-1
$$

$$
EAR = 0.0824
$$

> **Final Answer: 8.24%**

## 6. Common Mistakes

* ❌ Forgetting to divide the annual rate for monthly compounding.
* ❌ Forgetting to multiply years by the number of compounding periods.
* ❌ Confusing ordinary annuity with annuity due.
* ❌ Using the perpetuity formula for finite cash flows.
* ❌ Forgetting to discount deferred cash flows.

### PFAD

* **P** → Present Value
* **F** → Future Value
* **A** → Ordinary Annuity
* **D** → Annuity Due

Remember:

* Ordinary → End of period
* Due → Beginning of period

# Question 1 (Medium)

An engineer plans to purchase new software after 6 years. The software is expected to cost $25,000 at that time. If he can invest money today in an account earning 8% annually, how much money should he invest today to meet this goal?

---

## Step 1: Identify Data

We need money **today**.

Given:

Future Value:

$$
FV=25000
$$

Interest Rate:

$$
i=8%=0.08
$$

Years:

$$
n=6
$$

Need:

$$
PV=?
$$

---

## Step 2: Formula

Present Value:

$$
PV=\frac{FV}{(1+i)^n}
$$

---

## Step 3: Substitute

$$
PV=\frac{25000}{(1.08)^6}
$$

$$
PV=\frac{25000}{1.5869}
$$

$$
PV=15753.36
$$

### Final Answer

The engineer should invest:

> **$15,753.36 today**

# Question 2 (Medium)

A manufacturing company deposits $3000 at the end of every year into a maintenance fund. The account earns 7% annual interest. Determine the amount accumulated after 8 years.

---

## Step 1: Identify

Repeated yearly payments.

This is an **Ordinary Annuity**.

Given:

$$
PMT=3000
$$

$$
i=0.07
$$

$$
n=8
$$

Need:

$$
FV
$$

---

## Formula

$$
FV=PMT\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}
$$

---

## Solution

$$
FV=3000\frac{(1.07)^8-1}{0.07}
$$

$$
FV=3000\frac{1.7182-1}{0.07}
$$

$$
FV=3000(10.259)
$$

$$
FV=30777
$$

### Final Answer

> **$30,777**

---

# Question 3 (Hard)

A student wants to create an education fund that will provide $5000 every year for 10 years starting one year from now. If the account earns 6% annually, how much money is required today?

---

## Identify

Repeated future payments.

We need money today.

This is **Present Value of an Ordinary Annuity**.

Given:

$$
PMT=5000
$$

$$
i=0.06
$$

$$
n=10
$$

Need:

$$
PV
$$

---

## Formula

$$
PV=PMT\frac{1-(1+i)^{-n}}{i}
$$

---

## Solution

$$
PV=5000\frac{1-(1.06)^{-10}}{0.06}
$$

$$
PV=5000(7.3601)
$$

$$
PV=36800.50
$$

### Final Answer

> **$36,800.50**

# Question 4 (Hard)

An engineer borrows $50,000 to purchase equipment. The loan carries a 9% annual interest rate and must be repaid in equal yearly installments over 5 years. Determine the annual payment.

---

## Identify

Loan repayment.

Need:

$$
PMT
$$

Given:

$$
PV=50000
$$

$$
i=0.09
$$

$$
n=5
$$

---

## Formula

$$
PMT=\frac{PV\times i}{1-(1+i)^{-n}}
$$

---

## Solution

$$
PMT=\frac{50000\times0.09}{1-(1.09)^{-5}}
$$

$$
PMT=\frac{4500}{0.3504}
$$

$$
PMT=12842.47
$$

### Final Answer

> **$12,842.47 per year**

# Question 5 (Hard)

A bank advertises an annual interest rate of 12% compounded monthly. Determine the effective annual rate.

---

## Identify

Need:

EAR

Given:

$$
r=12%=0.12
$$

$$
m=12
$$

---

## Formula

$$
EAR=\left(1+\frac{r}{m}\right)^m-1
$$

---

## Solution

$$
EAR=\left(1+\frac{0.12}{12}\right)^{12}-1
$$

$$
EAR=(1.01)^{12}-1
$$

$$
EAR=0.1268
$$

### Final Answer

> **12.68%**

# Question 6 (Exam-Tricky)

A machine will generate savings of $4000 every year for 6 years. The first saving occurs one year from today. If the minimum attractive rate of return is 10%, determine the present worth of these savings.

---

## Identify Keywords

"Every year"

"6 years"

"First saving occurs one year from today"

This means:

✅ Ordinary annuity

Need:

$$
PV
$$

Given:

$$
PMT=4000
$$

$$
i=0.10
$$

$$
n=6
$$

---

## Formula

$$
PV=PMT\frac{1-(1+i)^{-n}}{i}
$$

---

## Solution

$$
PV=4000\frac{1-(1.10)^{-6}}{0.10}
$$

$$
PV=4000(4.3553)
$$

$$
PV=17421.20
$$

### Final Answer

> **$17,421.20**
