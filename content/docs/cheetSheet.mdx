# 1. Time Value of Money (TVM)

## Remember

**Money today is worth more than money tomorrow.**

Variables:

- PV = Present Value
- FV = Future Value
- i = Interest rate
- n = Number of periods
- A = Equal periodic payment (annuity)

---



## Lump Sum Formulas

### Future Value

$$
FV = PV(1+i)^n
$$

Use when:

- Given money today
- Need future amount

---

### Present Value

$$
PV=\frac{FV}{(1+i)^n}
$$

Use when:

- Given future amount
- Need today's value

---

## Annuity Formulas


## 1. Ordinary Annuity

An ordinary annuity involves periodic payments made at the **end** of each period (e.g., end of the month or year).

* **Future Value ($F.V$):**

$$F.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ (1 + r)^n - 1 \right]$$


* **Present Value ($P.V$):**

$$P.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ 1 - \frac{1}{(1 + r)^n} \right]$$



---

## 2. Annuity Due

An annuity due involves periodic payments made at the **beginning** of each period. Because each payment has more time to earn interest, you multiply the ordinary annuity formulas by $(1 + r)$.

* **Future Value ($F.V$):**

$$F.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ (1 + r)^n - 1 \right] (1 + r)$$


* **Present Value ($P.V$):**

$$P.V = \frac{C}{r} \left[ 1 - \frac{1}{(1 + r)^n} \right] (1 + r)$$


---

## TVM Shortcut Memory

- Present → Future = Compounding
- Future → Present = Discounting

# 2. Depreciation

## Straight Line Method

### Annual Depreciation

$$
D=\frac{Cost-Salvage\ Value}{Useful\ Life}
$$

### Book Value

$$
BV=Cost-(Depreciation\times Years)
$$

---

## Declining Balance Method

### Annual Depreciation

$$
Depreciation=Book\ Value\times Rate
$$

Remember:

- Book value decreases every year
- Depreciation decreases every year

---

# 3. Capital Budgeting Techniques

## Net Present Value (NPV)

### Formula

$$
NPV=PV\ of\ inflows-Initial\ Investment
$$

Decision:

- `NPV > 0` → Accept
- `NPV < 0` → Reject
- `NPV = 0` → Indifferent

Best method overall.

---

## Internal Rate of Return (IRR)

Definition:

The interest rate at which:

$$
NPV=0
$$

Decision:

- `IRR > Required Return` → Accept
- `IRR < Required Return` → Reject

---

## Profitability Index (PI)

### Formula

$$
PI=\frac{PV\ of\ inflows}{Initial\ Investment}
$$

Decision:

- `PI > 1` → Accept
- `PI < 1` → Reject
- `PI = 1` → Indifferent

Best for:

- Capital rationing
- Limited budget

---

## Payback Period

### Formula

$$
Payback=\frac{Initial\ Investment}{Annual\ Cash\ Inflow}
$$

Decision:

- Smaller payback period = Better

Weaknesses:

- Ignores TVM
- Ignores cash flows after payback

---

## Accounting Rate of Return (ARR)

### Formula

$$
ARR=\frac{Average\ Annual\ Profit}{Average\ Investment}\times100
$$

Decision:

- Higher ARR = Better

Weakness:

- Ignores TVM

---

# 4. Risk and Return

## Expected Return

$$
Expected\ Return=\sum(Probability\times Return)
$$

## Risk

Risk = Uncertainty

Remember:

- Higher risk → Higher potential return
- Lower risk → Lower potential return

---

## Investor Types

### Risk Averse

- Low risk
- Stable returns

### Risk Neutral

- Balanced risk and return

### Risk Seeking

- High risk
- High return

---

## Diversification

Definition:

Investing in multiple assets to reduce risk.

Remember:

Diversification reduces risk but cannot eliminate all risk.

---

# 5. Economic Efficiency

Measures whether benefits exceed costs.

### Formula

$$
Economic\ Efficiency=\frac{Worth}{Cost}
$$

Decision:

- Greater than 1 → Efficient
- Equal to 1 → Break-even
- Less than 1 → Inefficient

---

# 6. Technical Efficiency

Definition:

Producing maximum output using minimum input.

Remember:

- Less waste
- Better productivity
- Maximum output using minimum resources

Usually conceptual (no standard formula).

---

# 7. Cost-Volume-Profit (CVP) Analysis

## Contribution

$$
Contribution=Sales-Variable\ Cost
$$

or

$$
C=S-VC
$$

---

## P/V Ratio

$$
P/V\ Ratio=\frac{Contribution}{Sales}\times100
$$

---

## Break-Even Point (Units)

$$
BEP=\frac{FC}{SP-VC}
$$

Where:

- FC = Fixed Cost
- SP = Selling Price
- VC = Variable Cost per unit

---

## Break-Even Point (Sales Value)

$$
BEP=\frac{FC}{P/V\ Ratio}
$$

---

## Margin of Safety (Value)

$$
MOS=Actual\ Sales-BEP\ Sales
$$

---

## Margin of Safety (Profit)

$$
MOS=\frac{Profit}{P/V\ Ratio}
$$

---

## Target Sales (Units)

$$
Target\ Sales=\frac{FC+Target\ Profit}{SP-VC}
$$
