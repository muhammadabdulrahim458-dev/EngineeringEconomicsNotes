## 1. Economics

- **Definition:** Economics is the science of production, consumption, and distribution of goods and services.
- **Economic Goals:**
  1. High employment
  2. Price stability
  3. Efficiency
  4. Equitable income distribution
  5. Growth

---

## 2. Flow in an Economy

- **Households** provide land, labor, and capital.
- **Businesses** produce goods and services.
- **Money flows** between households and businesses in exchange for factors of production and finished goods.
- **Key Concept:** Interdependence between the two major entities (Households and Firms).

---

## 3. Law of Supply and Demand

- **Price Decrease:**
  - Demand **increases**.
  - Supply **decreases**.
- **Equilibrium Point:** The price at which **Demand = Supply**.
- **Factors Influencing Demand:** Income, prices of related goods, tastes and preferences.
- **Factors Influencing Supply:** Cost of production, technology, weather conditions.

---

## 4. Concept of Engineering Economics

- **Engineering:** The application of science for practical purposes.
- **Engineering Economics:** A branch that helps minimize costs and maximize benefits in engineering projects.

### Types of Efficiency

| Efficiency Type | Definition | Formula |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Technical Efficiency** | Efficiency in physical terms (output/input). | $TE = \frac{\text{Physical Output}}{\text{Physical Input}}$ |
| **Economic Efficiency** | Efficiency in monetary terms. | $EE = \frac{\text{Worth of Output}}{\text{Cost of Input}} > 100\%$ |

> **Exam Trick:** An economically efficient project always has a ratio **greater than 1 (or 100%)**.

---

## 5. Elements of Costs

### Cost Classification

1. **Variable Cost (Direct Cost):** Costs that vary with production level.
   - Direct Material
   - Direct Labor
   - Direct Expenses
2. **Overhead Cost (Indirect Cost):** Costs not directly traceable to production.
   - Factory Overheads (e.g., rent, utilities)
   - Administration Overheads (e.g., office salaries)
   - Selling Overheads (e.g., advertising)
   - Distribution Overheads (e.g., transportation)

### Cost Sheet Flow (Selling Price Calculation)

| Step | Cost Element | Accumulated Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Direct Material + Direct Labor + Direct Expenses | **Prime Cost** |
| 2 | Prime Cost + Factory Overheads | **Factory Cost** |
| 3 | Factory Cost + Administration Overheads | **Cost of Production** |
| 4 | Cost of Production + Selling & Distribution Overheads | **Cost of Sales** |
| 5 | Cost of Sales + Profit | **Sales** |

---

## 6. Other Costs and Revenues (Important Theory)

| Concept | Definition | Exam Significance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Marginal Cost** | The cost of producing one additional unit. | Used in decision-making. |
| **Marginal Revenue** | The extra revenue from selling one additional unit. | Used to maximize profit. |
| **Sunk Cost** | A cost that has already been incurred and cannot be recovered. | **Irrelevant** for future decisions. |
| **Opportunity Cost** | The return foregone from the best alternative investment. | Always considered in engineering economics. |

---

## 7. Break-Even Analysis

- **Break-Even Point (BEP):** The point where total revenue equals total cost (No profit, No loss).
- **Contribution ($C$):** The amount available to cover fixed costs and generate profit.
  $$ C = \text{Sales} - \text{Variable Costs} $$

- **Margin of Safety (MOS):** The excess of actual sales over break-even sales.
  $$ MOS = \text{Actual Sales} - \text{Break-Even Sales} $$

---

### BEP Formula (Units)

$$ \text{BEP (Units)} = \frac{\text{Fixed Cost}}{\text{Selling Price per Unit} - \text{Variable Cost per Unit}} $$

---

### Solved Numerical 1: Break-Even Point

**Question:** A company produces pens. Fixed cost is Rs. 10,000 per month. Variable cost per pen is Rs. 5. Selling price per pen is Rs. 15. Find the break-even point in units.

**Step 1:** Identify data.
- $FC = 10,000$
- $SP = 15$
- $VC = 5$

**Step 2:** Apply formula.
$$ BEP = \frac{10,000}{15 - 5} = \frac{10,000}{10} = 1,000 \text{ units} $$

> **Final Answer:** 1,000 units.

---

### Solved Numerical 2: Break-Even Point with Profit Target

**Question:** A company wants to earn a profit of Rs. 2,00,000. Fixed cost is Rs. 3,00,000. Selling price per unit is Rs. 500. Variable cost per unit is Rs. 300. How many units must be sold?

**Step 1:** Contribution per unit = $500 - 300 = 200$

**Step 2:** Required sales in units:
$$ \text{Units} = \frac{FC + \text{Target Profit}}{C} = \frac{300,000 + 200,000}{200} = \frac{500,000}{200} = 2,500 \text{ units} $$

> **Final Answer:** 2,500 units.

---

## 8. Profit/Volume Ratio (P/V Ratio)

- **P/V Ratio:** The ratio of contribution to sales. It measures the profitability of a product.

$$ \text{P/V Ratio} = \frac{\text{Contribution}}{\text{Sales}} \times 100 $$

- **Alternate Formulas:**
  $$ \text{BEP (Value)} = \frac{\text{Fixed Cost}}{\text{P/V Ratio}} $$
  $$ \text{Margin of Safety (Value)} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{P/V Ratio}} $$

---

### Solved Numerical 3: P/V Ratio and BEP

**Question:** Sales = Rs. 2,00,000. Variable Costs = Rs. 1,20,000. Fixed Costs = Rs. 60,000.
1. Calculate P/V Ratio.
2. Calculate Break-Even Sales (in Rs.).
3. Calculate Margin of Safety.

**Step 1:** Contribution = $2,00,000 - 1,20,000 = 80,000$

**Step 2:** P/V Ratio = $(80,000 / 2,00,000) \times 100 = 40\%$

**Step 3:** BEP (Value) = $60,000 / 0.40 = 1,50,000$

**Step 4:** Margin of Safety = $2,00,000 - 1,50,000 = 50,000$

> **Final Answer:** P/V Ratio = **40%**, BEP = **Rs. 1,50,000**, MOS = **Rs. 50,000**.

## 9. Formula Revision Sheet

| Concept | Formula |
| :--- | :--- |
| Contribution | $C = S - VC$ |
| BEP (Units) | $\frac{FC}{SP - VC}$ |
| BEP (Value) | $\frac{FC}{\text{P/V Ratio}}$ |
| P/V Ratio | $\frac{C}{S} \times 100$ |
| Margin of Safety (Value) | Actual Sales - BEP Sales |
| Margin of Safety (Profit) | $\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{P/V Ratio}}$ |
| Target Sales (Units) | $\frac{FC + \text{Target Profit}}{SP - VC}$ |
| Economic Efficiency | $\frac{\text{Worth}}{\text{Cost}} > 1$ |

| If the question says... | Think about... |
| :--- | :--- |
| "How many units to sell to break even?" | BEP (Units) = $FC / (SP - VC)$ |

## 12. MCQs (Self-Check)

1. If Price decreases, Demand:  
   a) Decreases  b) Increases  c) Stays same  d) None  
   **Ans: b**

2. Break-Even Point is where:  
   a) Total Cost > Total Revenue  b) Total Cost = Total Revenue  c) Total Cost < Total Revenue  d) None  
   **Ans: b**

3. Contribution is calculated as:  
   a) Sales - Fixed Cost  b) Sales - Variable Cost  c) Fixed Cost + Profit  d) Both b and c  
   **Ans: d**

4. P/V Ratio is also known as:  
   a) Profitability Index  b) Contribution Ratio  c) Net Profit Ratio  d) None  
   **Ans: b**

5. If P/V Ratio is 40% and Fixed Cost is Rs. 80,000, BEP (Value) is:  
   a) Rs. 32,000  b) Rs. 80,000  c) Rs. 2,00,000  d) Rs. 3,20,000  
   **Ans: c** ($80,000 / 0.40 = 2,00,000$)

6. Which cost is considered irrelevant for future decisions?  
   a) Marginal Cost  b) Opportunity Cost  c) Sunk Cost  d) Variable Cost  
   **Ans: c**

7. Economic Efficiency is measured by:  
   a) Output/Input  b) Worth/Cost  c) Profit/Sales  d) FC/VC  
   **Ans: b**

## 13. Common Mistakes (Exam Traps)

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Approach |
| :--- | :--- |
| Including Sunk Cost in future decisions. | Always **ignore** Sunk Cost. |
| Confusing Marginal Cost with Variable Cost. | MC is the cost of *one extra unit*, not total VC. |
| Forgetting to convert P/V Ratio to decimal in BEP formula. | If P/V Ratio is 40%, use 0.40 in BEP value formula. |
| Using Contribution incorrectly in P/V Ratio. | Contribution = Sales - Variable Cost (not Fixed Cost). |
| Thinking Equilibrium means Price is zero. | Equilibrium is where Demand = Supply (market clearing price). |

## 14. Memory Tricks (Mnemonics)

- **"V-F-A"** for Overheads:
  - **V** = Variable (Direct)
  - **F** = Factory
  - **A** = Administration/Selling

- **"P-C-O-S"** for Cost Flow:
  - **P**rime Cost
  - **C**ost of Production
  - **O**verheads
  - **S**ales

- **"SOMS"** for BEP concepts:
  - **S**ales
  - **O**pportunity (Cost)
  - **M**argin of Safety
  - **S**unk (Ignore)
