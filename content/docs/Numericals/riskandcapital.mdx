## Question 1: Expected Return (Single Stock)

> A stock has the following possible returns:
> - Boom: Probability 0.25, Return 25%
> - Normal: Probability 0.50, Return 15%
> - Recession: Probability 0.25, Return -10%
> 
> **Required:** Calculate the expected return.

---

### Step 1: Identify Data

- Boom: $P_1 = 0.25$, $R_1 = 25\%$
- Normal: $P_2 = 0.50$, $R_2 = 15\%$
- Recession: $P_3 = 0.25$, $R_3 = -10\%$

### Step 2: Apply Formula

$$ E(R) = \sum (R_i \times P_i) $$

### Step 3: Substitute and Solve

$$ E(R) = (25\% \times 0.25) + (15\% \times 0.50) + (-10\% \times 0.25) $$

$$ E(R) = 6.25\% + 7.50\% - 2.50\% = 11.25\% $$

> **Final Answer: 11.25%**

## Question 2: Expected Return, Variance & Standard Deviation

> | Probability | Return |
> | :--- | :--- |
> | 0.20 | 5% |
> | 0.50 | 10% |
> | 0.30 | 20% |
> 
> **Required:**
> 1. Calculate expected return.
> 2. Calculate variance.
> 3. Calculate standard deviation.

---

### Part 1: Expected Return

$$ E(R) = (0.20 \times 5\%) + (0.50 \times 10\%) + (0.30 \times 20\%) $$

$$ E(R) = 1\% + 5\% + 6\% = 12\% $$

> **Expected Return = 12%**

---

### Part 2: Variance

*Formula:* $\sigma^2 = \sum (R_i - E(R))^2 \times P_i$

| Return ($R_i$) | Deviation $(R_i - 12)$ | Squared | × Probability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 5% | -7 | 49 | 49 × 0.20 = 9.8 |
| 10% | -2 | 4 | 4 × 0.50 = 2.0 |
| 20% | 8 | 64 | 64 × 0.30 = 19.2 |

$$ \sigma^2 = 9.8 + 2.0 + 19.2 = 31.0 $$

> **Variance = 31.0**

---

### Part 3: Standard Deviation

$$ \sigma = \sqrt{31.0} \approx 5.57\% $$

> **Standard Deviation = 5.57%**

---

## Question 3: Coefficient of Variation (CV)

> - Investment A: Expected Return = 12%, Standard Deviation = 8%
> - Investment B: Expected Return = 18%, Standard Deviation = 15%
> 
> **Required:** Calculate coefficient of variation and identify the less risky investment.

---

### Step 1: Formula

$$ CV = \frac{\sigma}{E(R)} $$

### Step 2: Calculate CV for Both

**Investment A:**
$$ CV_A = \frac{8}{12} = 0.667 $$

**Investment B:**
$$ CV_B = \frac{15}{18} = 0.833 $$

### Step 3: Compare

- Lower CV means **less risk per unit of return**.
- $CV_A (0.667) < CV_B (0.833)$

> **Final Answer:** Investment A is less risky (has lower risk per unit of return).

## Question 5: Payback Period (Equal Cash Flows)

> - Initial Investment = Rs. 250,000
> - Annual Cash Inflow = Rs. 50,000
> 
> **Required:** Calculate the payback period.

$$ PBP = \frac{\text{Initial Investment}}{\text{Annual Cash Inflow}} $$

## Question 6: Payback Period (Unequal Cash Flows)

> - Initial Investment = Rs. 180,000
> - Year 1 = Rs. 40,000
> - Year 2 = Rs. 50,000
> - Year 3 = Rs. 60,000
> - Year 4 = Rs. 70,000
> 
> **Required:** Calculate the payback period.

---

### Step 1: Cumulative Cash Flow Table

| Year | Cash Flow | Cumulative CF | Remaining |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 0 | - | -180,000 | - |
| 1 | 40,000 | -140,000 | Pending |
| 2 | 50,000 | -90,000 | Pending |
| 3 | 60,000 | -30,000 | Pending |
| 4 | 70,000 | +40,000 | Recovered |

### Step 2: Calculate Fraction

- Amount needed at start of Year 4 = **Rs. 30,000**
- Cash flow in Year 4 = **Rs. 70,000**
- Fraction = $30,000 / 70,000 = 0.428$

$$ PBP = 3 + 0.428 = 3.43 \text{ years} $$

> **Final Answer: 3.43 Years**

## Question 7: Net Present Value (NPV)

> - Initial Investment = Rs. 200,000
> - Discount Rate = 10%
> - Year 1 = Rs. 80,000
> - Year 2 = Rs. 90,000
> - Year 3 = Rs. 70,000
> 
> **Required:** Calculate NPV.

---

### Step 1: Formula

$$ NPV = \sum \frac{CF_t}{(1+r)^t} - I_0 $$

### Step 2: Calculate PV of Each Cash Flow

- $PV_1 = 80,000 / (1.10)^1 = 80,000 / 1.10 = 72,727.27$
- $PV_2 = 90,000 / (1.10)^2 = 90,000 / 1.21 = 74,380.17$
- $PV_3 = 70,000 / (1.10)^3 = 70,000 / 1.331 = 52,592.04$

### Step 3: Sum PVs and Subtract Investment

$$ \text{Total PV} = 72,727.27 + 74,380.17 + 52,592.04 = 199,699.48 $$

$$ NPV = 199,699.48 - 200,000 = -300.52 $$

> **Final Answer: Rs. -300.52 (Negative, Reject)**

## Question 9: IRR Using PV Factor Tables

> - Initial Investment = Rs. 160,000
> - Annual Cash Inflow = Rs. 40,000
> - Project Life = 5 years
> 
> **Required:** Estimate IRR using PV factor tables.

---

### Step 1: Find PVIFA

$$ \text{PVIFA} = \frac{\text{Initial Investment}}{\text{Annual Cash Inflow}} = \frac{160,000}{40,000} = 4.00 $$

### Step 2: Look Up PVIFA Table (n = 5)

| Discount Rate | PVIFA (n=5) |
| :--- | :--- |
| 7% | 4.100 |
| 8% | 3.993 |

Our required PVIFA is **4.00**, which lies between 7% and 8%.

### Step 3: Interpolate

$$ IRR = 7\% + \frac{4.100 - 4.000}{4.100 - 3.993} \times (8\% - 7\%) $$

$$ IRR = 7\% + \frac{0.100}{0.107} \times 1\% = 7\% + 0.93\% = 7.93\% $$

> **Final Answer: ~7.93% (Approximately 8%)**

## Question 10: Comprehensive Capital Budgeting

> - Initial Investment = Rs. 500,000
> - Life = 5 years
> - Annual Cash Inflow = Rs. 140,000
> - Salvage Value = Rs. 50,000
> - Discount Rate = 12%
> 
> **Required:**
> 1. Payback Period
> 2. ARR (Accounting Rate of Return)
> 3. NPV
> 4. Profitability Index
> 5. Recommendation

---

### Part 1: Payback Period

$$ PBP = \frac{500,000}{140,000} = 3.57 \text{ years} $$

---

### Part 2: ARR (Accounting Rate of Return)

**Step 1:** Calculate Depreciation
$$ \text{Depreciation} = \frac{\text{Cost} - \text{Salvage}}{\text{Life}} = \frac{500,000 - 50,000}{5} = 90,000 $$

**Step 2:** Annual Net Profit
$$ \text{Annual Profit} = \text{Cash Inflow} - \text{Depreciation} = 140,000 - 90,000 = 50,000 $$

**Step 3:** Average Investment
$$ \text{Avg Investment} = \frac{\text{Cost} + \text{Salvage}}{2} = \frac{500,000 + 50,000}{2} = 275,000 $$

**Step 4:** ARR
$$ ARR = \frac{50,000}{275,000} \times 100 = 18.18\% $$

---

### Part 3: NPV

**Step 1:** PV of Annual Cash Inflows (PVIFA at 12%, n=5 = 3.6048)
$$ PV_{CF} = 140,000 \times 3.6048 = 504,672 $$

**Step 2:** PV of Salvage Value (PVIF at 12%, n=5 = 0.5674)
$$ PV_{SV} = 50,000 \times 0.5674 = 28,370 $$

**Step 3:** Total PV and NPV
$$ \text{Total PV} = 504,672 + 28,370 = 533,042 $$
$$ NPV = 533,042 - 500,000 = 33,042 $$

---

### Part 4: Profitability Index

$$ PI = \frac{533,042}{500,000} = 1.066 $$

---

### Part 5: Recommendation

| Criteria | Result | Decision |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Payback | 3.57 yrs | Acceptable |
| ARR | 18.18% | Good |
| NPV | Rs. 33,042 (Positive) | **Accept** |
| PI | 1.066 (> 1) | **Accept** |

> **Final Recommendation: ACCEPT THE PROJECT** (NPV > 0, PI > 1)

## Question 12: Advanced Risk and Capital Budgeting

> - Initial Investment = Rs. 800,000
> - Life = 5 years
> - Possible annual cash inflows:
>   - Probability 0.30 → Rs. 180,000
>   - Probability 0.50 → Rs. 220,000
>   - Probability 0.20 → Rs. 300,000
> 
> **Required:**
> 1. Expected annual cash inflow.
> 2. Expected NPV at 10%.
> 3. Discuss project risk.

---

### Part 1: Expected Annual Cash Inflow

$$ E(CF) = (0.30 \times 180,000) + (0.50 \times 220,000) + (0.20 \times 300,000) $$

$$ E(CF) = 54,000 + 110,000 + 60,000 = 224,000 $$

> **Expected Annual CF = Rs. 224,000**

---

### Part 2: Expected NPV (Discount Rate = 10%)

PVIFA at 10% for 5 years = 3.7908

$$ PV = 224,000 \times 3.7908 = 849,139.20 $$

$$ NPV = 849,139.20 - 800,000 = 49,139.20 $$

> **Expected NPV = Rs. 49,139 (Positive → Accept)**

---

### Part 3: Discuss Project Risk

- The cash inflows are **uncertain** (range from Rs. 180,000 to Rs. 300,000).
- The standard deviation of cash flows could be calculated, but qualitatively:
  - There is a **30% chance** of lower-than-average CF (180k).
  - There is a **20% chance** of much higher CF (300k).
- This variability creates **project risk**. The expected NPV is positive, but actual NPV could differ significantly.
- Management should consider **sensitivity analysis** or **scenario analysis** before finalizing.

> **Final Discussion:** The project has moderate risk due to varying cash flows, but expected NPV is positive. Proceed cautiously.

## Question 13: Simple Rate of Return

> Suppose you buy 10 shares of stock for $1000. The stock pays no dividend, but at the end of the year, you sell the stock for $1100. What is the rate of return on your investment?

---

### Step 1: Identify Data

- Beginning Value = $1,000
- Ending Value = $1,100
- Income = $0

### Step 2: Apply HPR Formula

$$ HPR = \frac{EV - BV + Income}{BV} = \frac{1100 - 1000 + 0}{1000} = \frac{100}{1000} = 0.10 $$

### Step 3: Convert to Percentage

$$ \text{Return} = 0.10 \times 100 = 10\% $$

> **Final Answer: 10%**

## Question 14: Expected Return and Risk

> An investment has:
> - 30% chance of producing a 25% return
> - 40% chance of producing a 10% return
> - 30% chance of producing a -15% return
> 
> **Required:** What is expected return? And how much risk does it have?

---

### Part 1: Expected Return

$$ E(R) = (0.30 \times 25\%) + (0.40 \times 10\%) + (0.30 \times -15\%) $$

$$ E(R) = 7.5\% + 4\% - 4.5\% = 7\% $$

> **Expected Return = 7%**

---

### Part 2: Risk (Standard Deviation)

**Step 1:** Deviations from $E(R) = 7\%$

| Return ($R_i$) | Deviation $(R_i - 7)$ | Squared | × Probability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 25% | 18 | 324 | 324 × 0.30 = 97.2 |
| 10% | 3 | 9 | 9 × 0.40 = 3.6 |
| -15% | -22 | 484 | 484 × 0.30 = 145.2 |

**Step 2:** Variance
$$ \sigma^2 = 97.2 + 3.6 + 145.2 = 246.0 $$

**Step 3:** Standard Deviation
$$ \sigma = \sqrt{246.0} \approx 15.68\% $$

> **Final Answer:**
> - Expected Return = **7%**
> - Risk (Standard Deviation) = **15.68%**
